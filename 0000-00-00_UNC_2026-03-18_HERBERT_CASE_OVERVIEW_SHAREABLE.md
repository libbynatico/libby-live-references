**3. THREE CORE FAILURES**

**Failure 1 --- Dr. Lawrence Green: Medication Mismanagement and
Battery**

Dr. Green assumed care July 5--11, 2025. During seven days he:

-   Introduced contraindicated medications (codeine, loperamide) for a
    patient with active Crohn\'s disease and stenosis history

-   Ignored a written Medication Refusal Statement delivered July 5,
    2025 --- citing the Health Care Consent Act

-   Broke a documented medication agreement --- codeine reappeared the
    morning after he agreed to Imodium-only

-   Set a clinically incorrect discharge threshold --- contradicting GI
    standards

-   Refused to order imaging while Matthew was deteriorating --- the CT
    ordered by Dr. Macleod the day Green left showed active obstruction
    and was flagged as critical

-   Attempted discharge while Matthew was in fetal position, crying,
    visibly obstructed

> *"Green refused to send me for a scan while I was crying in bed. It
> often felt like they didn\'t want proof on paper."*

Legal basis: Negligence + Battery (Malette v. Shulman, 1990, 72 O.R.
(2d) 417 --- written refusal ignored = battery regardless of medical
indication).

**Failure 2 --- CMH Institution: Discriminatory Discharge and Systemic
Neglect**

-   Discharged August 7 with no home care, no transport, no supplies ---
    into homelessness --- citing \'no fixed address\'

-   \'No fixed address\' denial was factually incorrect --- St. Mary\'s
    arranged full home care in 60 minutes on October 10

-   The contrast between CMH and St. Mary\'s demonstrates CMH\'s refusal
    was a discriminatory institutional choice --- not a policy
    constraint

-   Multiple ER visits post-discharge --- Matthew dismissed, not
    assessed, sent home without supplies or stabilization

-   November 10: left in feces for hours --- discharged soiled --- staff
    offered a plastic belongings bag as ostomy care

-   Pattern of failure documented from 2024--2025 --- not an isolated
    incident

Legal basis: Negligence + Ontario Human Rights Code (disability, receipt
of public assistance, housing status) + AODA violations + Punitive
damages (Whiten v. Pilot Insurance Co., 2002 SCC 18).
