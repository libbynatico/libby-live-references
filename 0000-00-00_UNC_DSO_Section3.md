**SECTION 3 -- Legislative and Policy Framework**

Ontario Regulation 276/10 outlines the statutory criteria for
determining whether an individual meets the definition of a
developmental disability.

**These criteria require:**

1\. Significant limitations in intellectual functioning

2\. Significant limitations in adaptive functioning

3\. Onset of these limitations prior to age 18

4\. Lifelong permanence

The regulation further requires the evaluator to apply a developmental
lens when interpreting adaptive functioning. This means that adult-onset
conditions, including traumatic brain injury, mental health issues, or
other medical complications, cannot be used to negate or overshadow
developmental impairments that originated in childhood or adolescence.

The DSO Policy Directive reinforces these requirements by mandating that
eligibility assessments include:

-- Developmental history from childhood

-- Academic and educational records

-- Collateral information where available

-- Evaluation of adaptive functioning across conceptual, social, and
practical domains

-- Consideration of long-standing patterns of impairment rather than
isolated adult presentation

These documents collectively establish that a valid developmental
disability determination must integrate historical evidence. The prior
assessment did not

adhere to these requirements, resulting in conclusions that do not
reflect the full scope of Mr. Herbert's developmental trajectory.
