**SECTION 10 -- Comprehensive Evidence Summary**

Section 10 provides a consolidated summary of the evidence establishing
that Mr. Herbert meets the definition of a developmental disability
under Ontario Regulation 276/10.

This section draws together findings from his developmental history,
adaptive functioning profile, educational trajectory, medical record,
and lifelong support needs.

10.1 Intellectual Functioning

Mr. Herbert demonstrates longstanding difficulty with:

-- Processing speed

-- Working memory

-- Learning new information

-- Abstract reasoning

-- Applying concepts across contexts

-- Maintaining attention during multi-step tasks

These indicators are consistent with intellectual impairment originating
in childhood, as documented in his school records and lifelong
functional patterns.

10.2 Adaptive Functioning

Significant limitations are present across all three domains:

Conceptual: difficulty with comprehension, reasoning,
instruction-following, and task sequencing.

Social: challenges interpreting cues, maintaining boundaries, expressing
needs clearly, and understanding expectations.

Practical: difficulty managing routines, organizing personal space,
navigating systems, budgeting, and performing multi-step daily tasks.

These deficits have persisted across all life stages.

10.3 Developmental Origin

Educational evidence shows clear impairment beginning in early
childhood.

Records from elementary through high school demonstrate consistent
difficulty meeting developmental expectations.

The requirement that impairments originate before age 18 is fully
satisfied.

10.4 Lifelong Nature of Impairments

Mr. Herbert's impairments have remained:

-- Persistent

-- Pervasive

-- Resistant to change

-- Present across settings

-- Functionally limiting in daily life

These characteristics meet the requirement that developmental
disabilities be lifelong.

10.5 Habilitative Support Needs

Across childhood, adolescence, and adulthood, Mr. Herbert has required
assistance to:

-- Acquire adaptive skills

-- Maintain routines

-- Interpret instructions

-- Navigate academic and bureaucratic environments

-- Complete multi-step tasks

-- Manage daily responsibilities

This history meets the habilitative support criterion in the regulation.

10.6 Distinguishing Co‑Occurring Conditions

While Mr. Herbert experienced a traumatic brain injury in adulthood, the
impairments documented throughout his schooling clearly predate this
injury.

The TBI compounded existing developmental impairments; it did not cause
them.

10.7 Alignment with O. Reg. 276/10

Based on the evidence across all domains, Mr. Herbert meets each of the
following criteria:

-- Significant limitations in intellectual functioning

-- Significant limitations in adaptive functioning

-- Impairments originating before age 18

-- Lifelong disability profile

This section demonstrates that the legislative definition is fully met.
