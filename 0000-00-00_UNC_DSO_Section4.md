**SECTION 4 -- Developmental History and Educational Evidence**

Mr. Herbert's developmental history demonstrates clear, measurable
impairments beginning well before the age of 18. These impairments were
consistently observable across academic, social, and practical domains.
His educational records provide objective evidence of long‑standing
limitations, independent of any adult medical conditions.

4.1 Early Childhood Indicators

Throughout elementary school, Mr. Herbert struggled with:

-- Reading comprehension

-- Written expression

-- Memory retention

-- Following multi‑step instructions

-- Completing tasks within expected timeframes

-- Understanding and retaining verbal explanations

Teacher comments frequently referenced difficulty keeping pace with
expectations and requiring repeated direction. These are hallmark
indicators of developmental impairment.

4.2 Middle School and Early Adolescence

As academic demands increased, Mr. Herbert's challenges became more
pronounced. Records show:

-- Difficulty organizing school materials

-- Struggles with sequencing tasks

-- Inconsistent academic performance

-- Trouble with transitions and adapting to new expectations

-- Reliance on teachers for repeated clarification

-- Slow acquisition of new concepts despite effort

These patterns reflect stable, long‑standing deficits---not temporary or
situational barriers.

4.3 High School Records

High school documentation demonstrates persistent and widening academic
gaps. Across Grades 9--12, Mr. Herbert:

-- Consistently scored below grade‑level expectations

-- Required significant teacher support to complete assignments

-- Demonstrated slow work pace and difficulty applying concepts

-- Displayed impaired executive functioning (planning, initiation,
follow‑through)

-- Encountered substantial difficulty with conceptual subjects including
mathematics, language, and sciences

These records provide objective, contemporaneous evidence of
developmental impairment during adolescence.

4.4 Delayed Graduation

Mr. Herbert required a fifth year to complete high school (Class of 2007
instead of 2006). This delay was not caused by absenteeism, behavioural
issues, or personal choice. It is consistent with:

-- Cognitive processing limitations

-- Difficulty meeting academic milestones

-- Impaired ability to manage academic workload

-- Persistent adaptive functioning challenges

Delayed graduation is a significant developmental red flag and directly
supports eligibility under O. Reg. 276/10.

4.5 Summary of Educational Trajectory

Mr. Herbert's academic record demonstrates:

-- Early developmental impairment

-- Persistent learning challenges

-- Difficulty acquiring and applying knowledge

-- Significant adaptive functioning limitations

-- Inability to meet developmental expectations without support

-- A longstanding pattern consistent with a developmental disability

These findings confirm that Mr. Herbert's impairments originated well
before age 18 and have remained lifelong in nature.
