**APPENDIX A -- Legislative & Policy Definitions**

**A-1 O. Reg. 276/10 -- Definition of Developmental Disability**

Ontario Regulation 276/10 defines a developmental disability as
requiring all of the following:

-- Significant limitations in intellectual functioning

-- Significant limitations in adaptive functioning

-- Onset of these limitations before age 18

-- Lifelong permanence

Adaptive functioning must be interpreted across conceptual, social, and
practical domains, using a developmental lens.

Co-occurring medical conditions do not negate or override developmental
impairments.

**A-2 SIPDDA Legislative Requirements**

The Services and Supports to Promote the Social Inclusion of Persons
with Developmental Disabilities Act (SIPDDA) requires:

-- Eligibility decisions to be accurate and evidence-based

-- Consideration of the individual's entire developmental trajectory

-- Assessment of functioning in real-world contexts

-- Fair access to services for individuals with lifelong impairments

These provisions form the foundation of all DSO eligibility decisions.

**A-3 DSO Policy Directive on Eligibility Determination**

DSO Policy mandates the following:

-- Review of childhood developmental history

-- Collection of school records where available

-- Evaluation of adaptive functioning using a developmental framework

-- Consideration of collateral sources

-- Integration of lifelong functional patterns

A developmental disability determination cannot rely solely on adult
presentation.

**A-4 Adaptive Functioning Definitions**

Adaptive functioning includes three domains:

Conceptual: memory, language, reading, reasoning, problem-solving,
executive functioning

Social: social judgment, communication, understanding cues,
interpersonal skills

Practical: daily living tasks, personal care, organization, budgeting,
routine management

Mr. Herbert has longstanding impairments in all three domains.

**A-5 DSM-5 Developmental vs Acquired Condition Crosswalk**

DSM-5 differentiates between:

-- Intellectual Developmental Disorder (lifelong)

-- Neurodevelopmental Disorders (lifelong)

-- Neurocognitive Disorders (acquired conditions such as TBI)

Key points:

-- Developmental disabilities often go unidentified in childhood

-- Individuals may appear articulate despite significant adaptive
impairment

-- Developmental and acquired impairments can coexist

-- Developmental origin must be established before age 18

Mr. Herbert's developmental history clearly aligns with a developmental
disability profile.
