**LEGAL FRAMEWORK -- MAY vs SHALL**
===================================

**Definitions**
---------------

**"May"** -- In Ontario statute, "may" is permissive and discretionary.
It empowers the decision-maker to act but does not require them to. The
decision-maker has the legal authority to choose.

**"Shall" / "Must"** -- In Ontario statute, "shall" and "must" are
mandatory and imperative. The decision-maker has no choice. The action
is required.

**Governing Legislation**
-------------------------

  **SOURCE**                          **EXACT LANGUAGE**                                                                                                                                     **KEY WORD**         **TYPE**
  ----------------------------------- ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ -------------------- -----------------------
  HTA s. 32(5)                        \"The Minister may issue a driver's licence to any person who meets the requirements\...\"                                                             \"may\"              DISCRETIONARY
  O. Reg. 341/94 s. 2(1)              \"An applicant\...may be issued a new licence without taking the applicable examinations\...if he or she held a licence\...within the three years.\"   \"may\"              PERMISSIVE EXEMPTION
  Photo Card Act s. 13(2)             \"No person who holds a valid driver's licence shall apply for\...a photo card.\"                                                                      \"shall not\"        MANDATORY PROHIBITION
  ontario.ca Photo Card page          \"If you want to re-apply\...you may need to take all the tests\...\"                                                                                  \"may need to\"      DISCRETIONARY
  ontario.ca -- 1--3 years invalid    \"You do not have to complete the graduated licensing system.\"                                                                                        \"do not have to\"   FULL EXEMPTION
  ontario.ca -- 3--10 years invalid   \"You are not required to serve the mandatory waiting periods between tests.\"                                                                         \"not required\"     FAST-TRACK
  HTA s. 47(1)                        \"The Registrar may suspend or cancel\...for any sufficient reason.\"                                                                                  \"may\"              DISCRETIONARY

**The Critical Finding**
------------------------

**No provision in the Highway Traffic Act, O. Reg. 340/94, O. Reg.
341/94, or the Photo Card Act contains the words "must restart at G1" or
"shall complete the graduated licensing system" for a driver in these
circumstances.**

The tiered invalidity framework on ontario.ca is Ministry operational
policy, not codified law. Operational policy is subject to
administrative discretion and can be departed from in exceptional
circumstances.
