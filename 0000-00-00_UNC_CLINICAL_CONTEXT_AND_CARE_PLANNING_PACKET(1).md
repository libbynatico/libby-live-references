***Authority and Legal Basis***

Under the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act and the
Ontario Human Rights Code, healthcare providers have a duty to provide
accessible services and accommodations for persons with disabilities, up
to the point of undue hardship. The Integrated Accessibility Standards
Regulation (O. Reg. 191/11) requires that accessible formats and
communication supports be provided in a timely manner and at no greater
cost than those provided to others. The Ontario Human Rights Commission
affirms that the duty to accommodate requires that barriers be removed
or avoided so that persons with disabilities can participate equally and
benefit equally from services. This document operationalizes those
obligations for this patient.
