**3. What the Law Actually Says**
---------------------------------

**The government's own website uses the word "may."** Ontario.ca states:
"you may need to take all the tests, serve mandatory wait times." The
word "may" is not "must." It indicates the Ministry has discretion.

**Highway Traffic Act, s. 32(5):** "The Minister may issue a driver's
licence to any person who meets the requirements of this Act." This is
discretionary authority.

**The Ministry's own reinstatement policy (3--10 years invalid):**

> *"You will have to complete the graduated licencing system. You must
> successfully complete a vision screening test, a knowledge test, and
> the G1 exit and G2 exit road tests. You are not required to serve the
> mandatory waiting periods between tests."*

Source: ontario.ca/page/reinstate-suspended-drivers-licence

**This means:** Even under the most restrictive reading, I can take all
four tests back to back. I could hold a full G licence again within
weeks. I do not need to wait 8--12 months at G1 and 12 months at G2.

**Ontario Regulation 341/94, s. 2(1):** "An applicant for a Class G
driver's licence may be issued a new licence without taking the
applicable examinations \[\...\] if he or she held a licence of the same
class applied for that was valid at any time within the three years
before making the application."

**No provision in the HTA, O. Reg. 340/94, O. Reg. 341/94, or the Photo
Card Act mandates a full G1 restart for a previously fully licensed
driver whose licence was cancelled through photo card issuance.**
