**1.1 Critical Interpretive Principle**

\~ RECALLED (confirmed principle, source: Rizzo v Rizzo Shoes \[1998\] 1
SCR 27, referenced in ODSP legal commentary)

\"It is a standard and well-established principle of interpreting
\'benefits-conferring\' legislation (such as the ODSP Act) that any
ambiguity in the legislation flows in favour of the benefits-claimant.\"
Practical use: When a caseworker interprets a policy narrowly to deny a
benefit, and the policy language is genuinely ambiguous, the law
requires interpretation in your favour. Cite this principle explicitly
in internal review requests.

**3. Assistive Devices Program (ADP)**

Administered by: Ministry of Health

Primary source:
https://www.ontario.ca/document/assistive-devices-program-health-care-professionals

ADP Policies and Procedures Manual (July 2023):
https://files.ontario.ca/moh-adp-policies-and-procedures-manual-assistive-devices-program-2023-07-01.pdf

**Confidence: ✓ CONFIRMED -- fetched this session**

  ---------------------------------------- ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Topic**                                **Confirmed Policy Content**
  **Objective**                            Provide consumer-centred support and funding to Ontario residents with long-term physical disabilities to access personalized assistive devices appropriate to their basic needs.
  **Eligibility -- Core Rule**             Long-term physical disability. Device must be for basic needs. Device CANNOT be required exclusively for sports, work, or school.
  **Funding -- Standard Model**            ADP pays 75% of vendor\'s selling price up to a maximum limit. Remaining 25% is co-payment (covered by ODSP/OW as mandatory benefit for recipients).
  **ODSP/OW Recipients -- Special Rule**   ADP pays 100% of ADP-approved price or maximum amount. Source: ontario.ca ADP health care professionals page.
  **Assessment Fee**                       ADP does not cover assessment fees. ODSP covers assessment fee if no other funding source. Fee cannot be reimbursed if device was purchased before ADP approval.
  **Replacement Schedule**                 ADP sets category-by-category replacement schedules. Early replacement requires separate justification. Repairs not covered by ADP -- covered by ODSP (mandatory for principal mobility device).
  **Excluded Devices**                     Devices intended solely for work, school, or recreational purposes.
  **Device Categories**                    Mobility aids, visual/hearing aids, orthotics/prosthetics, speech devices, medical supplies, environmental controls, respiratory devices, ostomy supplies, insulin syringes (seniors), enteral feeding pump/supplies, breast prostheses. Over 8,000 separate items.

**5. ODSP Employment Supports -- Governing Principles**

Source:
https://www.ontario.ca/document/ontario-disability-support-program-policy-directives-employment-supports/preamble

**Confidence: ✓ CONFIRMED -- fetched this session**

  ------------------------------ ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Principle**                  **Policy Language (Confirmed)**
  **Mandate**                    Connects people with disabilities to sustainable, competitive employment by providing supports to get, keep, and advance in a job.
  **Interpretation Principle**   \"The ODSP Act, regulations and policies should be interpreted flexibly and broadly\" in keeping with the spirit of the legislation.
  **Discretion**                 \"ODSP employment supports legislation and policies allow for discretion in some areas. In keeping with the spirit of the legislation, ministry staff should use this discretion to maximize the benefit that clients receive.\"
  **Individual Needs**           \"ODSP employment supports will be provided based on the client\'s individual needs and circumstances.\"
  **Barrier Removal**            \"Consistent with the intent of the Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (2005), barriers to accessing ODSP employment supports must be removed or minimized.\"
  **Client Rights**              Clients must be provided with clear and up-to-date information. Staff must recognize all clients as individuals with unique circumstances and unique barriers.

**6. Appeals and Rights Framework**

  ------------------------------------ ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **Mechanism**                        **Key Rule**
  **Internal Review**                  Must be requested first, before SBT appeal. \~ RECALLED -- verify specific timelines with local office.
  **Social Benefits Tribunal (SBT)**   Established under OW Act 1997. Hears appeals of ODSP and OW decisions. ODSP Act s.29: Tribunal cannot make a decision the Director has no authority to make. ✓ CONFIRMED -- CanLII source.
  **Divisional Court**                 Appeal from SBT on a question of law only (ODSP Act s.31). ✓ CONFIRMED -- CanLII source.
  **Yatar-JR (2024)**                  Following Yatar v. TD Insurance (SCC 2024): persons may also commence judicial review for fact-finding and mixed findings of fact and law, merged with court appeal in Divisional Court. \~ RECALLED -- verify current procedures as this is developing.
  **After Leaving ODSP**               Rapid Reinstatement available. In most cases only financial eligibility needs to be re-established; medical forms not required. ODSP Directive 1.3. ✓ CONFIRMED -- in directive index.
