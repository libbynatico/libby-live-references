**Work Capacity**

In my clinical opinion, Mr. Herbert is not capable of sustaining
substantially gainful employment at this time. The combination of
permanent physical limitations, executive dysfunction, auditory
processing disorder, unpredictable gastrointestinal function, and
post-surgical recovery creates a cumulative functional profile that
precludes reliable employment participation.

Specifically: he cannot tolerate sustained seated positioning due to
permanent abdominal structural changes. He cannot safely use a standard
lap seatbelt. He cannot reliably process verbal instructions in a work
environment due to APD. He cannot maintain the sequencing and task
completion demands of most employment roles due to executive
dysfunction.

These limitations directly prevent his return to his prior occupation as
an AZ commercial driver, which was his primary employment for 21 years.

**PERMANENCE AND PROGNOSIS**

Mr. Herbert\'s impairments are chronic and permanent. The ileostomy is
irreversible. The post-surgical abdominal deformity is permanent. The
TBI sequelae --- executive dysfunction, short-term memory impairment,
auditory processing disorder --- are not expected to significantly
improve. The combined functional profile is unlikely to change in a
manner that would restore his capacity for substantially gainful
employment.
