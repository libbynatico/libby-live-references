**APPENDIX B -- Psychological Assessment Analysis**

**B-1 Extracted Findings from Assessment**

The psychological assessment identified several impairments, including:

-- Difficulty with working memory

-- Impaired processing speed

-- Challenges following multi-step directions

-- Executive functioning limitations

-- Social vulnerability

-- Difficulty expressing needs clearly

-- Reliance on external supports

However, these findings were interpreted as primarily the result of
adult-onset conditions without adequate consideration of Mr. Herbert's
documented developmental history.

**B-2 Contradictions & Omissions in the Assessment**

The assessment contains several contradictions:

-- "Strong insight" vs. "limited insight"

-- "Independent functioning" vs. "significant support reliance"

-- "Adequate comprehension" vs. "difficulty following instructions"

Key omissions include:

-- No developmental history

-- No school records reviewed

-- No childhood functioning considered

-- No habilitative support needs evaluated

-- No developmental framework applied

These omissions directly undermine the validity of the conclusions.

**B-3 Intellectual vs Developmental Interpretation Errors**

The assessment relied heavily on adult testing results and isolated
strengths, failing to account for:

-- Lifelong patterns of impairment

-- Childhood academic records

-- Developmental deficits documented before age 18

-- The requirement to interpret adaptive functioning developmentally

This resulted in attributing impairments to adult conditions rather than
acknowledging their developmental origin.

**B-4 Executive Functioning Evidence**

Executive functioning deficits documented include:

-- Difficulty initiating tasks

-- Problems sequencing steps

-- Impaired judgment

-- Poor working memory

-- Difficulty shifting attention

-- Challenges with planning and prioritization

These impairments were present long before adulthood and are consistent
with a developmental disability profile.

**B-5 Adaptive Functioning Interpretation Errors**

Adaptive functioning was assessed primarily based on adult presentation.
This approach failed to:

-- Consider developmental origin

-- Review childhood adaptive functioning

-- Integrate functional patterns across life stages

-- Evaluate conceptual, social, and practical domains holistically

These errors conflict with O. Reg. 276/10 and accepted standards for
developmental disability evaluation.
