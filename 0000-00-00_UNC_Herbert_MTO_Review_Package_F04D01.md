**The Critical Legal Finding: No Statute Mandates G1 Restart**
--------------------------------------------------------------

No provision in the Highway Traffic Act, Ontario Regulation 340/94,
Ontario Regulation 341/94, or the Photo Card Act, 2008 contains the
words \'must restart at G1\' or \'shall complete the graduated licensing
system\' for a driver in Matthew Herbert\'s circumstances. The
government\'s own published guidance uses \'may\' - not \'shall\' or
\'must.\' This creates space for administrative discretion.

The tiered invalidity framework published on ontario.ca (1-3 years /
3-10 years / 10+ years) is Ministry operational policy, not codified
law. Operational policy is by definition subject to administrative
discretion and can be departed from in exceptional circumstances. A
21-year commercial driver with zero demerit points, zero at-fault
collisions, and documented extenuating circumstances represents
exceptional circumstances.

**Question 2 - Challenge the G1 Restart Mandate**
-------------------------------------------------

> *\"Can you identify the specific section of the Highway Traffic Act or
> Ontario Regulation 340/94 or 341/94 that mandates a G1 restart for a
> driver in these circumstances - specifically the word \'shall\' or
> \'must\' requiring graduated restart? The ontario.ca photo card page
> uses \'may need to\' - not \'must.\'\"*
>
> **ontario.ca Photo Card page and O. Reg. 341/94, s. 2(1)**

Why this works: No such \'shall\' or \'must\' exists. The regulation
creates permissive exemptions - it says what CAN be waived. Its silence
about persons outside the window does not create a mandatory restart.
The supervisor will need to escalate to find the answer.

**Question 3 - Identify the Review Pathway**
--------------------------------------------

> *\"Given that the Ministry\'s own reinstatement policy for 3-10 year

>
> **ontario.ca Reinstatement page - 3-10 year category**

Why this works: Even accepting the most restrictive Ministry reading,
the 3-10 year policy explicitly removes mandatory waiting periods. This
opens the door to requesting full waiver of testing for a professional
driver with a clean safety record.
