**Re: Clinical Recommendation for Passport Funding and Associated
Supports\
Prepared for: Developmental Services Ontario**

**Based on my involvement in Matthew Herbert's care and my review of his
functional, medical, and cognitive history, it is my clinical opinion
that Matthew requires Passport-funded supports immediately to maintain
basic health, safety, and stability in the community. His profile
demonstrates severe adaptive functioning impairment across conceptual,
social, and practical domains. These impairments are longstanding,
consistent, and significantly exacerbated by his chronic medical
conditions, traumatic brain injury, and current homelessness.**

**Matthew presents with profound difficulty in the following areas:**

-   **Executive functioning: impaired planning, organization,
    > sequencing, and task initiation.**

-   **Working memory and auditory processing: difficulty understanding,
    > retaining, and acting on verbal information without structured
    > repetition or written aids.**

-   **Daily living skills: challenges with hygiene routines, meal
    > preparation, medical supply management, and maintaining a safe
    > living environment.**

-   **Health and safety: high-output ostomy, dehydration risk, chronic
    > fatigue, and limited capacity to self-monitor early signs of
    > medical deterioration.**

-   **Community navigation: inability to manage applications,
    > appointments, transportation, or bureaucratic systems without
    > direct support.**

**These impairments place Matthew at elevated risk of repeated medical
crises, loss of housing, and inability to meet basic needs
independently.**

**Passport-funded supports would provide:**

-   **Structured daily living assistance to compensate for his practical
    > skill deficits.**

-   **System navigation support to prevent further loss of critical
    > services due to cognitive impairment.**

-   **Accompanied community participation to reduce isolation and
    > support mental and physical health.**

-   **Support worker assistance during high-risk medical periods to
    > ensure safety and prevent hospitalization.**

-   **Technology and planning supports to address significant memory and
    > processing limitations.**

**In my clinical judgment, Passport services are not optional for
Matthew---they are medically and functionally necessary. Without
structured, consistent support, he is unlikely to achieve or maintain
housing stability, community safety, or adequate health management. His
upcoming major surgical intervention further increases his vulnerability
and makes the need for coordinated supports urgent.**

**I strongly recommend expedited approval for developmental services and
immediate access to Passport-funded supports to stabilize Matthew's
functioning and mitigate ongoing risk.**
