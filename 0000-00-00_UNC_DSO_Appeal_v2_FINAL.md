**1. What This Appeal Is About**
================================

On November 14, 2025, Matthew Herbert underwent a psychological
assessment for DSO eligibility conducted by Lisa Sedore. Despite the
assessor's own findings documenting severe and pervasive functional
limitations, the final conclusion stated that Matthew "does not meet
criteria for a developmental disability."

This appeal demonstrates that the determination was incorrect. It was
based on a narrow interpretation of the assessment data, procedural
errors that excluded critical evidence, and a misapplication of the
statutory criteria under the Services and Supports to Promote the Social
Inclusion of Persons with Developmental Disabilities Act, 2008 (SIPDDA).

**The assessor's own data, when properly applied to the statutory
criteria, supports eligibility. This appeal asks DSO to re-determine
eligibility based on the full evidentiary record.**

**2.2 --- The ABAS-3 Scores Are Below the 1st Percentile in Every Domain --- And Were Dismissed**
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Adaptive Behavior Assessment System (ABAS-3) was completed by
Kellsie Hnatejko, Matthew's primary caregiver, who has direct daily
knowledge of his functioning. The results were unambiguous:

**Every single domain fell in the Extremely Low range, below the 1st
percentile.**

This means Matthew functions below 99% of the general population in
every area of adaptive behaviour the instrument measures --- including
communication, community use, home living, health and safety, self-care,
self-direction, social skills, and functional academics.

The assessor acknowledged these scores but then **discounted them**
because "medical issues contribute to the limitations." This reasoning
is legally incorrect under SIPDDA.

**SIPDDA, s. 3(1)--(2), explicitly allows medical and acquired causes**
that arise before age 18. Matthew's traumatic brain injury occurred at
age 12. His Crohn's disease was diagnosed at age 13. Both are
developmental-age events. The statute does not require that the cause be
congenital or genetic. It requires that the condition originated before
18 and results in significant lifelong limitations.

**The assessor applied a disqualifier that the statute does not support.
The ABAS-3 scores meet the adaptive functioning threshold. Full stop.**

**2.3 --- The Assessment Conditions Were Inaccessible and the Results Were Misinterpreted**

Furthermore:

**SIPDDA requires assessment of real-world adaptive functioning, not
performance on isolated tasks in a controlled environment.**

**4. The Statutory Test and How It Is Met**
===========================================

Under SIPDDA, O. Reg. 276/10, eligibility requires three criteria plus
permanence:

  **Criterion**         **Requirement**
  --------------------- -------------------------------------------------------------------------
  **1. Onset**          Significant limitations originated before age 18
  **2. Intellectual**   Significant limitations in intellectual functioning
  **3. Adaptive**       Significant limitations in adaptive functioning across multiple domains
  **4. Permanence**     Expected to continue indefinitely

------------------------------------

**TBI at age 12 (2001):** Described by treating physician as moderate
concussion with mild brain damage and permanent short-term memory loss.
See B-19 and B-30.

**Crohn's disease at age 13 (2002):** Incurable. Led to permanent
ileostomy at age 24.

**School records (A-1 through A-54):** Impairment documented from Grade
2 --- before the TBI. The brain injury compounded a pre-existing
vulnerability.

**Onset before 18 is met through multiple independent lines of
evidence.**

**5. Specific Procedural and Interpretive Errors**
==================================================

**1. Exclusion of school records:** The assessor told Matthew not to
bring documents. The report cited insufficient evidence of early
impairment. 54 school records spanning Grade 2 through Grade 12 now
demonstrate onset well before age 18 --- with specific teacher comments
documenting attention, organization, processing, and task-completion
deficits across 15 years and dozens of independent observers.

**2. Dismissal of ABAS-3 scores:** Below the 1st percentile in every
domain. Dismissed because "medical issues contribute." SIPDDA explicitly
allows medical causes before age 18.

**3. Overreliance on composite FSIQ:** FSIQ of 92 masks specific
deficits at the 9th and 16th percentiles. SIPDDA does not specify an IQ
cutoff.

**4. Misinterpretation of arrival:** Arriving on time was treated as
independence. The caregiver took time off work, physically helped him
out of bed, and drove him.

**5. No accommodation for APD:** The assessment relied on verbal
instructions. Matthew's nodding during the session reflects lip-reading
and effortful processing, not reliable comprehension. Apparent
engagement should not be equated with understanding.

**6. Vyvanse and optimal conditions:** Stimulant medication plus a
quiet, one-on-one room produces medicated peak performance. This does
not reflect unmedicated baseline daily functioning across the demands of
real life.

**7. No reading tested:** Reading comprehension --- one of the subject's
most functionally limiting deficits --- was not assessed.

**8. Compounding not assessed:** TBI + Crohn's + APD + chronic pain +
executive dysfunction + ADHD + cognitive fatigue + ostomy management = a
level of impairment exceeding the sum of individual conditions.

**9. Absence of records ≠ absence of impairment:** The report treated
missing early documentation as evidence that impairment didn't exist. It
actually reflects a homeless person's inability to locate childhood
records --- records that now exist.

**10. Controlled testing vs. real life:** The subject has been
essentially self-reliant since age 18 --- not because he is capable of
independence but because no support structure existed. He finished high
school while working 40+ hours per week. He taught himself to appear
functional because no one was going to help him. He has had over 30 jobs
since age 14. This is not evidence of capacity. It is evidence of the
absence of support.
