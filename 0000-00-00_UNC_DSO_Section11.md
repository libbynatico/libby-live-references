**SECTION 11 -- Request for Reconsideration**

Section 11 outlines the formal request for reconsideration, based on the
corrected developmental interpretation and the complete evidence
presented in this binder.

11.1 Reconsideration of Eligibility

Mr. Herbert respectfully requests that Developmental Services Ontario
conduct a full reconsideration of his eligibility determination under O.
Reg. 276/10.

The evidence demonstrates that his impairments:

-- Originated before age 18

-- Have remained lifelong and pervasive

-- Significantly impact adaptive functioning across conceptual, social,
and practical domains

-- Require ongoing habilitative support

-- Meet all legislative criteria for a developmental disability

11.2 Review of Submitted Documentation

This binder provides:

-- Educational records

-- Developmental summaries

-- Functional evidence

-- Analysis of assessment deficiencies

-- Clarification of habilitative support history

-- Legislative and policy references

DSO is respectfully asked to review all materials in Appendices A
through E as part of the reconsideration process.

11.3 Clarification of Requirements

If any further documentation, explanation, or collateral information is
needed, Mr. Herbert is willing to provide it.

He requests that any required clarification be communicated clearly, in
writing, and with specific details so he can meaningfully respond.

11.4 Written Justification if Eligibility Is Denied

Should eligibility remain denied following reconsideration, Mr. Herbert
respectfully requests a written explanation outlining:

-- Which specific criteria were considered unmet

-- What evidence was deemed insufficient

-- What additional documentation or assessments would be required

This ensures procedural fairness and allows him to understand the basis
for the decision.

11.5 Recognition of Barriers

Mr. Herbert emphasizes that navigating these processes is significantly
more difficult for him due to his developmental and cognitive
impairments.

He requests that, in accordance with DSO policy, the reconsideration
process take into account:

-- His documented communication barriers

-- His difficulty interpreting complex instructions

-- His reliance on external support to understand administrative
requirements

These considerations are relevant to ensuring equitable access to the
system.

11.6 Statement of Good Faith

This request is made respectfully and in good faith.

Mr. Herbert's goal is not to challenge the assessor personally but to
ensure that the legislative criteria are applied accurately and that the
decision reflects the full developmental record.
