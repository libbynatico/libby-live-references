**IV. THE MAY 2020 CHARGES AND COVID-ERA LICENSING EXTENSIONS**

Regarding the improper licence class: I hold an AZ licence. My
commercial driver\'s medical certificate had expired due to an
administrative quirk --- my D endorsement was obtained one year before
my A endorsement, creating an earlier renewal cycle. I received no
notice that my licence class had been downgraded.

On March 20, 2020, the Province of Ontario filed Ontario Regulation
73/20 under the Emergency Management and Civil Protection Act,
retroactive to March 16, 2020. That regulation suspended the running of
all time periods and limitation periods established by Ontario statutes,
regulations, and orders --- including the Highway Traffic Act and its
regulations. Ontario Regulation 340/94, which governs commercial driver
medical reporting requirements, is made under the Highway Traffic Act.
The medical reporting deadlines for commercial drivers therefore fell
within the scope of the emergency suspension.

Separately, the Ministry of Transportation communicated through industry
channels that commercial driver medical certificates expired after March
1, 2020 were extended. These extensions were renewed repeatedly ---
through August, September, and November 2020, January 2021, and
ultimately June 2021 --- before a formal sunset date of October 31, 2021
was announced. The MTO recommended that drivers carry a copy of the
extension memo as proof. The US Federal Motor Carrier Safety
Administration formally recognized Ontario\'s extensions.

It is also relevant that MTO\'s licence class database did not always
reflect the extension in real time. In some cases, the system had
already triggered a class downgrade because the medical appeared
overdue, and the extension had not been applied retroactively in the
database. This meant that when police ran a licence at roadside, the
terminal could show a downgraded class even when the driver\'s legal
entitlement to operate had been preserved by the emergency order. This
is a documented administrative issue with the rollout of the COVID
extension policy, and it is precisely the situation that appears to have
occurred in this stop.

Most significantly: my own physician\'s office created an internal
record the day after the stop documenting exactly what had happened. The
note, dated May 27, 2020 and reproduced in full in Appendix A of this
submission, states that I called looking to book a driver\'s medical
exam because I had been pulled over the previous day by an officer who
\'apparently gave no sh\*ts that MOS are extending licenses / doctor\'s
offices are not doing these physicals,\' and that my licence had expired
right before everything shut down so I had not gone in to renew because
MTO was closed.

This is a contemporaneous, third-party professional record created one
day after the stop by clinic staff who had no motive to fabricate. It
corroborates that the extension was understood to be in effect, that MTO
offices were closed for in-person renewals, and that I attempted to
remedy the situation the morning after the stop. The improper class
charge should not have stood in light of the province\'s own regulatory
framework.

My employer, whose CVOR-registered vehicle I was operating, told me at
