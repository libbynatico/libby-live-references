**SECTION 2 --- RELEVANT ONTARIO LAWS AND POLICIES**

**2.1 --- Highway Traffic Act, R.S.O. 1990, c. H.8**

**Section 32(5) --- Ministerial Authority to Issue Licences**

This is the foundational provision. It states:

> *\"The Minister MAY issue a driver's licence to any person who meets
> the requirements of this Act and the regulations authorizing the
> person to drive on a highway, (a) any motor vehicle within a class or
> classes of motor vehicles; (b) subject to any conditions or
> endorsements\"*--- HTA s. 32(5)

**ANALYSIS: The word "may" is critical. This is permissive, not
mandatory language. It grants the Minister discretionary authority over
licence issuance, including the ability to impose or waive conditions.
The Minister is not required by statute to force graduated licensing on
a reinstating driver. The graduated licensing process is a regulatory
framework, not a statutory one.**

**Section 32(7) --- Reinstatement After Suspension**

States that a suspended licence is not valid until the prescribed
administrative fee has been paid. Mr. Herbert paid the full balance of
\$4,939.10 on March 12, 2026. This statutory condition has been
satisfied.

**Section 41 --- Suspension on Conviction**

Governs suspensions arising from HTA and Criminal Code convictions. Mr.
Herbert's DWS convictions fall under this section. The 6-month
suspension from the June 29, 2022 conviction (Suspension \#2065071)
expired December 29, 2022.

**Section 41.1 --- Reinstatement, Reduction, Extension**

Provides that where the Registrar is satisfied that the prescribed
assessments and programs have been completed, the Registrar SHALL
reinstate the licence upon suspension expiry. Note the mandatory "shall"
language --- once conditions are met, reinstatement is not
discretionary.

**Section 46 --- Suspension for Unpaid Fines**

This is the provision under which Mr. Herbert's licence was first
suspended in September 2021. The suspension under s.46 ends when the
fines are paid. Payment was made March 12, 2026.

**Section 53 --- Driving While Under Suspension**

**SECTION 3 --- KEY LEGAL DEFINITIONS AND QUOTES**

**3.1 --- "May" vs. "Shall"**

"May" in HTA s.32(5) grants discretionary authority. "Shall" in HTA
s.41.1 imposes mandatory obligation. The Minister has discretion in how
licences are issued and under what conditions. This means the graduated
licensing reset is an administrative policy decision, not a statutory
requirement --- and policy decisions are subject to accommodation
obligations under the Ontario Human Rights Code.

**3.2 --- Ontario Human Rights Code --- Duty to Accommodate**

> *\"Under the Code, employers and unions, housing providers and service
> providers have a legal duty to accommodate the needs of people with
> disabilities who are adversely affected by a requirement, rule or
> standard.\"*--- OHRC Policy on Ableism and Discrimination Based on
> Disability, s. 8

The Ontario Human Rights Code has quasi-constitutional primacy over
Ontario legislation. ServiceOntario and the Ministry of Transportation
are service providers under the Code. Their reinstatement process must
accommodate persons with disabilities to the point of undue hardship.

**3.3 --- Procedural Duty to Accommodate**

> *\"A failure to give any thought or consideration to the issue of
> accommodation, including what, if any, steps could be taken
> constitutes a failure to satisfy the procedural duty to
> accommodate.\"*--- Cited in OHRC Policy, s. 8

**APPLICATION: If MTO applies the graduated licensing reset to Mr.
Herbert without considering his disability, his documented inability to
manage administrative processes, and the circumstances under which his
licence lapsed, this constitutes a procedural failure to accommodate
under the Code.**

**3.4 --- AODA Primacy Clause**

The AODA states that where it conflicts with another Act or regulation,
the provision providing the highest level of accessibility shall
prevail. However, where the AODA falls short of the Human Rights Code,
the Code prevails. This means the MTO cannot rely on O. Reg. 340/94 as a
defence for applying graduated licensing requirements that have a
discriminatory effect on persons with disabilities.

**4.2 --- Ministerial Discretion Under HTA s.32(5)**

The Minister's authority to issue licences is permissive ("may"). This
includes the power to impose conditions or endorsements. By extension,
it includes the power to waive or modify conditions where justified. A
formal request to the Minister or the Registrar for a discretionary
reinstatement pathway, supported by medical evidence and driving
history, is a legitimate avenue.

**4.3 --- The Driver Improvement Interview**

MTO Driver Control may require attendance at a Driver Improvement
interview as part of reinstatement. Mr. Herbert has already stated
willingness to complete any required interview, course, or
re-examination. The interview process is itself discretionary and allows
MTO to assess the driver's fitness individually rather than applying a
blanket graduated licensing requirement.

**4.4 --- Canadian Armed Forces Exemption (By Analogy)**

O. Reg. 340/94 s.12(3) exempts Canadian Armed Forces members and former
members from certain graduated licensing requirements when applying for
Class A, C, D, or F licences if they meet the criteria under O. Reg.
341/94 s.6. While Mr. Herbert is not a military member, this provision
demonstrates that the regulatory framework already contemplates
exemptions for experienced drivers. The principle --- that prior
demonstrated competence can reduce reinstatement requirements --- is
directly transferable as an argument for equitable treatment.

**4.5 --- COVID-19 Emergency Extensions**

Ontario extended licence validity during COVID-19. Mr. Herbert's licence
shows an expiry of November 29, 2024. Under COVID extensions, licences
that expired on or after March 1, 2020 were extended until February 28,
2022. This may affect the calculation of when the licence became
