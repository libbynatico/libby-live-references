**SECTION 6 -- Origin of Impairments Prior to Age 18**

Ontario Regulation 276/10 requires that developmental disabilities
originate before the age of 18.

Mr. Herbert's developmental record clearly demonstrates early onset of
cognitive and adaptive impairments.

6.1 Documented Childhood Impairments

Mr. Herbert's elementary and middle school records show:

-- Difficulty meeting core academic milestones

-- Impaired reading comprehension

-- Slow acquisition of new concepts

-- Trouble following instructions

-- Early signs of executive functioning deficits

-- Challenges with organization and working memory

These impairments did not appear suddenly in adulthood but were
consistently present throughout his youth.

6.2 Persistent Impairments Through Adolescence

Throughout high school, Mr. Herbert continued to exhibit:

-- Below-grade-level academic performance

-- Difficulty with problem-solving and abstract reasoning

-- Slow work pace

-- Frequent need for clarification and teacher guidance

-- Difficulty completing assignments without support

-- Inconsistent retention of course material

These patterns indicate impairments consistent with a neurodevelopmental
profile.

6.3 Delayed Educational Progression

Mr. Herbert required an additional year to complete high school.

This delay is a significant indicator of developmental impairment.

It reflects:

-- Difficulty keeping pace academically

-- Challenges managing increasing academic demands

-- Inability to independently meet developmental milestones

The extended timeframe is consistent with developmental disability.

6.4 Distinguishing Developmental Origin from Adult Injury

While Mr. Herbert later experienced an adult traumatic brain injury, the
TBI did not create his impairments.

It exacerbated existing challenges that were present since childhood.

His developmental trajectory---including early academic struggles,
impaired adaptive functioning, and persistent difficulty across learning
domains---predates his adult medical conditions.

6.5 Lifelong Nature of Impairments

Developmental disabilities are lifelong.

Mr. Herbert's impairments:

-- Originated in childhood

-- Persisted through adolescence

-- Continue into adulthood

-- Have remained stable and pervasive, regardless of environment

This fulfills the legal requirement for developmental origin under O.
Reg. 276/10.
