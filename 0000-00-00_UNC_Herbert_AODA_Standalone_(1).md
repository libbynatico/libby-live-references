**Medical Basis for This Request**
==================================

Matthew Herbert sustained a traumatic brain injury (TBI) at age 12 in
2001. This injury has resulted in permanent, documented auditory
processing disorder (APD) and executive dysfunction. These conditions
have been confirmed across multiple clinical assessments spanning
childhood through adulthood.

**What Auditory Processing Disorder Means in Practice**
-------------------------------------------------------

APD is not a hearing volume problem. Matthew can hear sound at normal
levels. His audiometric testing shows adequate volume detection.
However, his brain cannot reliably interpret, organize, and retain
spoken language --- particularly when the information is complex,
delivered quickly, in a noisy environment, or under cognitive load.

This means:

> • Complex administrative, legal, or policy information communicated
> verbally may not be fully understood or retained
>
> • Busy service environments (counters, waiting rooms, phone calls)
> significantly reduce processing capacity
>
> • By the time he reaches a service counter after navigating transit
> and managing his medical conditions, his cognitive capacity may
> already be depleted
>
> • He may appear to understand when he does not --- APD is an invisible
> disability

**Hearing Aid Situation**
-------------------------

Matthew was denied funded hearing aids because his condition is auditory
processing, not volume loss. APD does not meet Ontario criteria for
funded hearing devices. He has self-funded Apple AirPods as a
compensatory device for phone calls and in-person meetings. Government
systems that rely primarily on phone contact are functionally
inaccessible to him.

**Legal Basis**
===============

The AODA Customer Service Standard (O. Reg. 191/11) requires that
government service providers communicate with persons with disabilities
in a manner that accounts for their disability. The Ontario Human Rights
Code requires accommodation to the point of undue hardship. Providing
written communication is not an undue hardship for any government
office.

*This request applies to all government agencies, healthcare providers,
and service organizations interacting with Matthew Herbert's file. It is
a standing accommodation request and does not need to be re-submitted
for each interaction.*
