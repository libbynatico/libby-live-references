**APPEAL LETTER -- BLOCK 2 (Pages 2--5)**

1\. Purpose of This Submission

Mr. Herbert respectfully submits this formal appeal requesting a
reconsideration of the recent eligibility determination conducted under
Ontario Regulation 276/10 -- Services and Supports to Promote the Social
Inclusion of Persons with Developmental Disabilities Act (SIPDDA).

This section outlines the purpose of the appeal, including the need for
reconsideration based on developmental history, adaptive functioning
impairments, and the correction of interpretive errors in the prior
assessment.

2\. Background and Context of Assessment

Mr. Herbert underwent a psychological assessment intended to determine
eligibility for adult developmental services. Although the assessor
demonstrated professionalism, the assessment did not incorporate
essential developmental history, school records, or lifelong adaptive
functioning patterns. These omissions led to interpretive errors.

3\. Relevant Legislative Framework

Under O. Reg. 276/10, eligibility for developmental services requires:

-- Significant limitations in intellectual functioning

-- Significant limitations in adaptive functioning

-- Onset prior to age 18

-- Lifelong impairments

The policy also requires evaluators to examine the individual's
developmental history, including educational records and support needs.

4\. Correcting the Developmental History Record

Mr. Herbert's school records, academic trajectory, and functional
history demonstrate clear developmental impairments originating in early
childhood. He required five years to complete high school, consistent
with developmental delays. Academic struggles, impaired comprehension,
and adaptive functioning deficits were present long before adulthood.

5\. Distinguishing Developmental Disability from Adult TBI

The previous assessment incorrectly attributed impairments solely to Mr.
Herbert's adult traumatic brain injury. Evidence shows these impairments
were present throughout childhood and adolescence, with the TBI later
exacerbating, but not causing, the underlying developmental disability.
