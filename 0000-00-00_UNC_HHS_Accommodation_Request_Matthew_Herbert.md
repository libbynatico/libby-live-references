**Part 3: Legal and Policy Framework**

**Ontario Human Rights Code --- Duty to Accommodate**

Under the Ontario Human Rights Code, service providers --- including
hospitals --- have a legal duty to accommodate individuals with
disabilities to the point of undue hardship. This duty applies to
patients, visitors, and essential care partners. Key principles include:

-   The duty to accommodate applies to all \'social areas\' covered by
    the Code, including services and facilities such as hospitals.

-   Accommodation must be individualized --- there is no set formula.
    The most appropriate accommodation is the one that best meets the
    individual\'s actual needs.

-   An organization can only be exempt from providing accommodation if
    it would cause \'undue hardship\' --- a very high bar that considers
    only cost and health and safety, not inconvenience or administrative
    burden.

-   Under the OHRC, a person does not need to disclose their specific
    diagnosis --- only the functional limitations relevant to the
    accommodation being requested.

-   The Code also protects individuals associated with a person with a
    disability (e.g., a partner). Hospitals must accommodate \'family
    status\' considerations and the needs of essential care partners.

**Accessibility for Ontarians with Disabilities Act (AODA)**

Hamilton Health Sciences is subject to AODA requirements as a public
sector organization. HHS has published multi-year accessibility plans
(2020--2025 and 2026--2031) and explicitly recognizes its obligation to
accommodate patients with disabilities. Key points:

-   HHS\'s accessibility page states: \'You are welcome to bring a
    support person with you to the hospital. A support person is an
    individual hired or chosen to accompany you to help with
    communication, mobility, personal care, medical needs or access to
    services as it relates to your disability.\'

-   HHS\'s Patient Experience team (ext. 75240) is identified as the
    channel for accessibility needs: \'Please contact our Patient
    Experience team at 905-521-2100 ext. 75240 to let us know how we can
    meet your needs.\'

-   HHS Accessibility Plan 2026--2031 acknowledges that \'barriers
    remain\' and commits to improving accessibility for patients with
    disabilities.

-   HHS also provides information in accessible formats upon request and
    has a process for receiving and addressing accessibility-related
    feedback through the Office of Patient Experience.

**Infection Control --- Clinical Basis for Private Room**

The request for a private postpartum room is not only a matter of
comfort or preference. There is a recognized clinical and infection
control basis for protecting immunocompromised individuals from
shared-room exposure in hospital settings:

-   Clinical infection control guidelines (CDC, 2007 Guideline for
    Isolation Precautions) recommend that patients on Droplet or Contact
    Precautions not be roomed with patients who are immunocompromised or
    have conditions that increase their risk of adverse outcomes.

-   Published peer-reviewed guidance on infection prevention for
    immunocompromised hosts identifies shared-room exposure as a risk
    factor for healthcare-associated infections (HAIs) in this
    population.

-   Clinical literature specifically recommends protective isolation
    considerations for immunocompromised individuals --- including
    single-room placement to reduce pathogen exposure from nearby
    patients and the hospital environment.

-   Matthew\'s intestinal failure, ostomy, surgical wound history, and
    dependence on nutritional support all represent compounding factors
    that elevate his infection susceptibility in a shared-room
    environment.

-   The postpartum ward is an environment where other patients may
    harbor common postpartum pathogens (e.g., GBS, Staphylococcus),
    respiratory viruses, or carry organisms that would pose minimal risk
    to healthy individuals but elevated risk to an immunocompromised
    support person sharing the space.

**HHS Preferred Accommodation --- Formal Process**

Hamilton Health Sciences has a documented Preferred Accommodation
Request (Pre-Admission Form), administered through Patient Accounts.
This form normally covers private room requests, typically framed as an
insured/uninsured upgrade. However, when the accommodation need arises
from a documented disability rather than personal preference, the legal
framing shifts from a billing/upgrade matter to a human rights
duty-to-accommodate matter. The request should therefore be flagged
through two parallel channels:

-   Clinical / Obstetrics team --- notation on the patient chart that a
    private postpartum room is requested on an accessibility basis, with
    partner disability documentation attached.

-   Patient Experience / Accessibility Office (ext. 75240) --- formal
    documentation of the accommodation request so it exists on record
    independent of room availability at the time of admission.

(private) accommodation as a billable upgrade (OHIP does not cover
private rooms as a standard benefit). However, when a private room is
needed to accommodate a documented disability --- either of the patient
or of their support person --- the legal basis shifts to a human rights
accommodation, not a billing matter.

-   Matthew Herbert has multiple documented disabilities on file within

    Diseases).

-   The request is made in advance with adequate lead time, allowing the
