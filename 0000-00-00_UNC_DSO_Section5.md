**SECTION 5 -- Adaptive Functioning Analysis**

Under Ontario Regulation 276/10, adaptive functioning must be evaluated
across three domains: conceptual, social, and practical.

Eligibility requires significant limitations in at least one of these
domains. Mr. Herbert demonstrates long-standing, pervasive limitations
across all three.

5.1 Conceptual Domain

This domain includes memory, language, reading comprehension, reasoning,
problem‑solving, and executive functioning.

Since childhood, Mr. Herbert has struggled with:

-- Retaining new information

-- Processing verbal and written instructions

-- Understanding multi-step tasks

-- Applying academic concepts

-- Sequencing information logically

-- Planning, organizing, and prioritizing tasks

These impairments are documented consistently throughout his schooling
and continue into adulthood.

5.2 Social Domain

This domain includes interpersonal judgment, understanding social cues,
communication clarity, and vulnerability to exploitation.

Mr. Herbert has historically experienced:

-- Difficulty interpreting tone, intent, and nuance

-- Trouble understanding expectations in social contexts

-- Frequent misunderstandings

-- Vulnerability in professional or bureaucratic interactions

-- Difficulty maintaining boundaries

-- Trouble advocating for himself in unfamiliar situations

These patterns were present in adolescence and persist today, reflecting
developmental origin.

5.3 Practical Domain

This domain includes personal care, daily routines, money management,
health and safety, and independent living skills.

Mr. Herbert's challenges include:

-- Difficulty managing daily routines without reminders

-- Trouble organizing personal space and belongings

-- Inability to manage schedules or deadlines independently

-- Difficulty completing multi-step daily tasks

-- Weakness in budgeting and planning

-- Difficulty navigating transportation or new environments

-- Reliance on others for medical, administrative, and logistical
assistance

These impairments significantly limit functional independence and align
with developmental disability criteria.

5.4 Developmental Origin of Adaptive Deficits

All three domains show clear impairment beginning before age 18.

These limitations are not attributable solely to adult-onset medical
conditions and cannot be explained by environmental or situational
factors.

5.5 Stability and Lifelong Nature of Impairments

Mr. Herbert's adaptive functioning limitations have remained:

-- Persistent

-- Longstanding

-- Resistant to change despite effort

-- Present across all major life stages

Together, these findings strongly support eligibility under O. Reg.
276/10.
