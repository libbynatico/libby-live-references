**Why a Tablet Is Required --- Summary of Documented Functional
Barriers**

-   Auditory processing disorder from TBI makes phone-only communication
    > unreliable --- verbal instructions cannot be processed and
    > retained in real time without visual support

-   Video-based communication via smartphone is not effective --- screen
    > size prevents facial cue recognition and lip-reading, which
    > Matthew relies on to supplement his auditory processing

-   During and after surgery, Matthew is bed-bound and cannot tolerate
    > prolonged upright posture --- a tablet enables communication and
    > document review from a lying or semi-reclined position

    > system --- continued use within the Apple ecosystem is a medically
    > justified accommodation, not a product preference

    > signing documents remotely) require a screen size that a
    > smartphone cannot provide without inducing visual fatigue and
    > cognitive overload

**Why This Falls Outside the Assistive Devices Program (ADP)**

The request explicitly addresses ADP applicability: ADP does not fund
multipurpose assistive technology used to address combined and
fluctuating cognitive, communication, visual-processing, and
fatigue-related impairments. There is no applicable ADP device category
or authorizing pathway for this accommodation. The OW discretionary
benefits stream is the correct and only appropriate channel for this
request.

**Accessories Requested**

-   Protective screen cover --- reduce glare and protect device during
    > frequent handling

-   Apple Pencil or equivalent stylus --- handwritten notes,
    > annotations, and digital signatures when typing is not feasible
    > due to fine motor fatigue

-   Durable protective case with integrated keyboard --- stable use from
    > bed or seated positions
