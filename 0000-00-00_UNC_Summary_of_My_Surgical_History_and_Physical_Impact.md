**Summary of My Surgical History and Physical Impact**

I have undergone major abdominal surgeries that have had profound and
lasting effects on my body, health, and daily life. These procedures are
complex, invasive, and come with significant challenges, many of which
are difficult to fully convey through words alone. Below is a detailed
description of what I have experienced.

**Total Proctocolectomy**

This surgery involved the complete removal of my colon and rectum. It
required multiple large abdominal incisions and extensive internal organ
manipulation. As a result, my digestive system was fundamentally
altered, and I now rely on an ileostomy---a surgically created opening
in my abdomen to allow waste to exit my body.

The surgery's complexity was very high (rated 9/10) due to the
involvement of multiple major organs and the creation of a permanent
ostomy. The invasiveness was equally significant (9/10), with deep
tissue trauma and large muscle disruption.

**Recovery and Complications**

The initial recovery was long and demanding, requiring at least 8 to 12
weeks of healing before regaining basic strength and mobility. Pain and
discomfort were severe and persistent during this period (8/10), and
managing the new ostomy presented additional ongoing challenges.

I experienced significant scarring both externally on my abdomen and
internally due to adhesions (rated 8/10). Mobility was temporarily and,
in some respects, permanently affected (7/10), limiting my ability to
perform daily activities without pain or fatigue.

**Ileostomy Resection Surgery**

Following the total proctocolectomy, I underwent an additional major
procedure to repair a severe parastomal hernia---an abdominal wall
defect near my ileostomy site that caused further complications. This
surgery added another layer of complexity, risk, and recovery demands to
my overall health situation.

**Risks and Long-Term Impact**

My risk of infection and blood loss during these surgeries was moderate
to high (7/10), requiring careful medical management and monitoring. The
impact on my future bodily function is permanent (rated 10/10), as the
removal of my colon and rectum, coupled with the ileostomy,
fundamentally changed my digestive processes.

**Psychological and Lifestyle Impact**

Beyond the physical challenges, these surgeries have deeply affected my
mental health and daily life (8/10). The permanent lifestyle
adjustments, body image changes, and ongoing medical care needs are
significant burdens I continue to manage.

This summary reflects the true scope of my surgical history and its
impact, which goes far beyond simple measures of recovery time or
visible scarring. It serves as a clear explanation of the challenges I
face every day as a direct result of these life-altering procedures.
