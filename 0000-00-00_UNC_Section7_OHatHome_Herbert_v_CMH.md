**DOCUMENTED WOUND CONDITIONS --- MARCH 10--11, 2026**

The Ontario Health atHome nursing records from March 10--11, 2026
document the following wound and skin conditions during the
post-surgical recovery period. These records are held by the patient as
physical documents and are to be inserted at this tab.

**1. Moisture-Associated Skin Damage (MASD)**

MASD is damage to the skin caused by prolonged exposure to moisture ---
in this context, wound exudate or ostomy output. In a post-surgical
patient with an open wound adjacent to an ileostomy, MASD represents a
significant complication risk. It compromises the skin\'s integrity,
increases infection risk, and impairs wound healing. In a patient
already documented as malnourished with compromised tissue healing
capacity, MASD is particularly serious.

The presence of MASD on March 10--11 --- approximately six weeks
post-surgery --- is consistent with a wound that had been inadequately
managed during the wound VAC delay period.

**2. Open Skin at Wound Site**

The nursing records document open skin at the wound site. This confirms
the wound had not closed at this stage of the post-surgical period. In
the context of the wound VAC delay documented in Section 6, open skin at
six weeks post-surgery is consistent with inadequate wound management
during the period when wound VAC therapy was not being provided due to
the HHS authorization failure.

**3. Inadequate Ostomy Supply Availability**

The nursing records also document inadequate ostomy supply availability
at the time of the visit. This is a separate failure from the wound
complications --- it reflects a breakdown in the supply chain supporting
a patient who requires daily ostomy management and is in active
post-surgical recovery.

The patient had been attempting to establish a stable monthly medical
supply arrangement since at least December 2025, as documented in
correspondence with Preston Medical Pharmacy. The supply shortage
documented in the March nursing records suggests these efforts had not
yet produced a reliable supply chain by the time of the visit.

**COMPOUNDING FACTOR --- OW TRANSPORTATION DENIAL**

On February 18, 2026 --- the date of the wound VAC and staple removal
appointment with Dr. Forbes --- Ontario Works caseworker Colleen
Brunelle denied transportation authorization for a taxi to the
appointment.

The stated reason for the denial was that the authorized taxi provider
(Red Top Taxi) requires a credit card, which the patient does not
possess. This is a documented systemic barrier, not a patient choice.
The patient has no driver\'s licence and no personal vehicle. Kellsie
Hnatejko, his support person, was pregnant throughout this period.

The patient attended the February 18 appointment by arranging
transportation at his own expense. This is a medically necessary
appointment for post-surgical wound management. The denial of
transportation to a post-surgical wound care appointment by the income
support program is documented and is relevant both to the damages
calculation and to the ongoing ODSP transition file.

> *This transportation denial is documented in the Disability Binder
> prepared March 9, 2026. It is one of multiple documented instances
> where administrative barriers compounded the medical harm Matthew
> Herbert was already experiencing as a result of the institutional
> failures documented in this binder.*
